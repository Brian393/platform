Williams Ave Community Garden

NYRP acquired the Williams Ave. Garden in 1999,preserving it as open space in perpetuity. NYRP received funding from thestate of NY to renovate the Williams Ave. Community Garden. Over the nextfew months, we will work with community members and local stakeholders todesign a plan for the space and begin construction soon after.

History

Formerly part of New Lots,East New York has been home to waves of immigrant groups: Eastern Europeans inthe 1800's and Puerto Ricans and African‐Americans from the south in the1900's. Today it has a growing South Asian population. The garden lot was aformer residence, transferred to the city in 1976. Adjacent buildings bothconstructed in 1901.

Climate forecasts

Rising temperatures

Mean annual temperatures are projected to increase by 4.1 to 5.7°F by the 2050s. The frequency of heat waves is projected to triple by the 2080s.

Rising sea levels and coastal flooding

Projections for sea level rise in New York City are 11 to 21inches by the 2050s, 18 to 39 inches by the 2080s, and could reach as high as 6feet by 2100. Projected sea level rise will increase current 100-year coastalfloods by 10-15 fold by the 2080s.

*Increased precipitation *

Mean annual precipitation is projected to increase by 4% to 11% bythe 2050s.

Source: NewYork City Panel on Climate Change 2015 Report

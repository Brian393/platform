Hull St Community Garden

NYRP acquired the Hull St. Garden in 1999, preserving it as open space in perpetuity. In 2017 NYRP received funding from the state of NY to renovate the Hull St. Community Garden. Over the next few months, we will work with community members and local stakeholders to design a plan for the space and begin construction soon after.

History

The Ocean Hill neighborhood was founded in 1890. Until the 1960's it was a mostly Eastern and Southern European low‐income immigrant community. Since the mid‐20th century, the neighborhood has been predominately African American. The lot was historically residential. Adjacent buildings both constructed in 1925.

Climate forecasts

Rising temperatures

Mean annual temperatures are projected to increase by 4.1 to 5.7°F by the 2050s. The frequency of heat waves is projected to triple by the 2080s.

Rising sea levels and coastal flooding

Projections for sea level rise in New York City are 11 to 21inches by the 2050s, 18 to 39 inches by the 2080s, and could reach as high as 6feet by 2100. Projected sea level rise will increase current 100-year coastalfloods by 10-15 fold by the 2080s.

Increased precipitation

Mean annual precipitation is projected to increase by 4% to 11% bythe 2050s.

Source: NewYork City Panel on Climate Change 2015 Report
